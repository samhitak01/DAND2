Introduction¶

In this project, I am analyzing the TMDB movie dataset which is stored in the "tmdb-movies.csv" file. I am interested in studying the data and understanding the trends. Specifically, I am trying to answer the following questions by processing the data accordingly:

  1. How has the number of movies made per year changed over the years? Has the number increased or decreased or remained about the same?
  2. What is the distribution of the movies made so far in terms of the genres? Which of the genres have the highest number of movies made?
  3. What is the relation between runtime of the movies and popularity? Is there a range of runtimes that the audience prefer?
  4. Who are the directors with the most number of popular movies? (Note: The criteria has to be decided first for considering a movie "popular". In other words, what is the minimum value of popularity over which movies are considered popular)

Conclusions¶

From the analysis performed on the given dataset, the following are my conclusions:

  1. The number of movies made per year seems to be consistently increasing. The highest number of movies made so far seem to be in the year 2014.
  2. From the distribution of the movies into their respective genres, it looks like Drama, Comedy and Thriller are the top 3 genres in terms of the number of total movies made over the years.
  3. From the runtime vs popularity graph, it appeared that the peak popularity occurs when the runtime is between 150 - 200 minutes which is basically 2 hours and 30 minutes to 3 hours and 20 minutes. It could be possible that the audience prefer this duration for watching a movie. Movies having higher or lower runtimes than this seem to be not so popular.
  4. In the list of movies deemed to be popular, the top director with the most number of movies is Woody Allen. Clint Eastwood, Steven Spielberg, Martin Scorsese and Ridley Scott rank next in order.
